How to Get Your Apple ID Password

If you've forgotten your Apple ID password, follow these methods to retrieve or reset it:

  • Use Apple's official password reset tool at iforgot.apple.com
  • Check if your password is saved in Keychain Access on your Mac
  • Look for password hints you may have set up previously
  • Check your email for any Apple communications containing password hints
  • Use two-factor authentication if enabled on your account
Security Note: Apple never stores passwords in plain text and cannot tell you your current password. You can only reset it to a new one.

Password Recovery Methods

1

Using Trusted Devices

If you have a trusted iPhone, iPad, or Mac, you can reset your password directly in Settings. Go to Settings > [your name] > Password & Security > Change Password and follow the prompts.

2

Account Recovery

If you can't access your trusted devices, use account recovery at iforgot.apple.com. This may take a few days as Apple verifies your identity to prevent unauthorized access.

3

Two-Factor Authentication

With two-factor authentication enabled, you can reset your password by receiving a verification code on your trusted phone number or device.

Password Recovery Help

Still can't get your password?

  • No Access to Recovery Email: You may need to go through account recovery verification
  • Forgot Security Answers: Contact Apple Support for assistance
  • Account Locked: Wait 24 hours or verify your identity to unlock
  • Trusted Device Not Available: Use account recovery process
  • Don't Remember Apple ID: Check previous purchases or emails from Apple
Important: Apple will never ask for your password via email, phone call, or text message. Always verify you're on the official Apple website before entering any credentials.
